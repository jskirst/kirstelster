Our Story

Once upon a time (ie 2006), Jonathan Kirst and Martha Elster were two kiddos about to graduate high school and leave their nests to go to college. Jonathan was a West Coast football player with hopes of a degree in computer programming and Martha was a Southern pre-med with hopes of a degree in science. Little did they know their paths would collide together in the middle of the country in a city neither really knew much about - St. Louis.

During their sophomore year at WashU, the two landed on the same dorm hallway at opposite ends. As they say, opposites attract and soon JC and Martha were friends. After several months of courtship (aka eating stirfry together at The Village and watching movies like Ratatouille in the common room) the two started dating at the close of sophomore year. Martha and JC found themselves inseperable for the remainder of college. They moved from eating stir fry at the cafeteria every night to learning how to cook together. They tried to take up each other's hobbies (Martha attended her first WashU football game in which she cheered for the wrong team..). And they travelled between coasts at every vacation.

After college, Martha moved back to the South for medical school and JC headed back to the West Coast for work. Since then, the two have been stealing time together whenever they can. As they prepare to become Mr. and Mrs. (and as Martha prepares to become Dr.), they are looking forward to a life together starting with residency next year.

The Peabody Opera House, originally named the Kiel Opera House, was built in 1932 as part of urban development plans sparked by the 1904 World's Fair in St. Louis. The Opera House was home to a main theater of 3,500 seats and an auditorium of 9,300 seats. The Kiel Opera House opened on April 21, 1934 with it's first show, Aida, hosting multiple stars from the New York Met. The Opera House carried on a strong tradition for the next fifty years with a resume ranging from serving as the home of the St. Louis Symphony to hosting musicians such as Elvis Presley, Billy Joel, and the Rolling Stones. The Kiel Opera House was closed in 1991 and was renovated and reopened in 2010 by the Peabody Energy Company under the new name of the Peabody Opera House.

St. Louis

Martha and Jonathan are excited to share their home away from home with you!

For those who are new to St. Louis and are planning to fly, the closest airport is Lambert St. Louis International (STL). Once at the airport, there are many options for transportation to downtown which is approximately 15 miles away. The MetroLink departs from the airport and will take guests downtown for approximately $3. Cabs can be easily taken from the airport to the hotels downtown for around $35-40. Many hotels also provide shuttles from the airport for a fee.

Martha and Jonathan are in the process of reserving a block of hotel rooms for wedding guests at the Hilton at the Ballpark in downtown St. Louis. They will update this section as they know more!
